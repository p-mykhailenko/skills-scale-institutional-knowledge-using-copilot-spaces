# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
Project Sponsors provide strategic direction, secure necessary resources, and ensure project alignment with business goals. They serve as the executive champion for the project and resolve major risks and blockers.

### Responsibilities
- Define and communicate strategic objectives and success criteria
- Secure funding, resources, and executive support
- Provide high-level oversight and governance
- Resolve escalated risks and organizational impediments
- Review and approve major milestones and deliverables
- Ensure alignment with organizational strategy and priorities

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers strategic business value
- Remove organizational barriers to success
- Maintain executive visibility and support
- Drive strategic decision-making and priority alignment

### Typical Communication
- Monthly steering committee meetings
- Executive status briefings and escalation reviews
- Strategic decision approvals and resource commitments
- Stakeholder alignment sessions

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Receives regular status updates, approves major decisions, helps remove blockers
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on strategic vision and business outcomes
- **Stakeholders**: Represents project interests at executive level, secures buy-in
- **Business Analyst**: Reviews business case and validates strategic alignment

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business requirements and technical implementation. They elicit, analyze, and document business needs, ensuring that development teams understand what to build and why.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Translate business needs into functional specifications
- Create use cases, user stories, and acceptance criteria
- Facilitate requirements workshops and validation sessions
- Ensure traceability between business needs and technical solutions

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear, complete, and testable
- Reduce rework by clarifying expectations upfront
- Facilitate shared understanding between business and technical teams
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Documentation of user stories and acceptance criteria
- Collaboration sessions with development teams
- Validation meetings with stakeholders and product owners

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on product vision and prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Provides requirements documentation and effort estimates
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements and answers technical questions
- **Quality Lead**: Defines test scenarios based on acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders**: Elicits needs and validates proposed solutions

---

## Quality Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Leads oversee all quality assurance activities, establish testing standards, and ensure deliverables meet quality criteria before release. They coordinate quality efforts across development and testing teams.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards, metrics, and acceptance criteria
- Plan and coordinate testing activities (functional, integration, performance)
- Review test coverage and identify quality risks
- Manage defect tracking and resolution priorities
- Conduct quality reviews and sign-off for releases
- Drive continuous improvement of quality processes

### Goals
- Ensure deliverables meet or exceed quality standards
- Identify and mitigate quality risks early
- Reduce production defects and customer-reported issues
- Foster a culture of quality throughout the development lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Daily coordination with testing and development teams
- Weekly quality metrics reviews with Project Manager
- Release readiness assessments and sign-offs
- Post-release quality retrospectives

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Reports quality status, risks, and readiness for release
- **Developers**: Collaborates on test strategy and defect resolution
- **Business Analyst**: Uses requirements to create test scenarios
- **Product Manager**: Validates quality standards align with customer expectations
- **Communications Coordinator**: Provides quality status for stakeholder updates

---

## Communications Coordinator

### Role Summary
Communications Coordinators ensure timely, accurate, and consistent communication across project teams and with external stakeholders. They manage communication plans, coordinate updates, and ensure all parties stay informed.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and execute project communication plans
- Coordinate status updates, announcements, and stakeholder briefings
- Manage project documentation repositories and knowledge sharing
- Facilitate effective meetings and ensure action items are tracked
- Monitor communication channels and escalate issues
- Ensure consistent messaging across all stakeholder groups

### Goals
- Maintain transparency and alignment across all stakeholders
- Prevent information gaps and communication breakdowns
- Ensure timely escalation of critical information
- Foster collaborative team culture through effective communication

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status report compilation and distribution
- Stakeholder newsletters and project announcements
- Meeting facilitation and minutes distribution
- Ad-hoc coordination for urgent communications

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Primary partner for status reporting and stakeholder updates
- **Project Sponsor**: Prepares executive briefings and strategic communications
- **All Team Members**: Gathers updates and facilitates information flow
- **Stakeholders**: Delivers regular updates and manages expectations
- **Change Manager**: Coordinates change-related communications

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers assess, plan, and facilitate organizational changes required for successful project adoption. They manage change requests, assess impacts, coordinate stakeholder engagement, and ensure smooth transitions.

### Responsibilities
- Assess change impact on processes, systems, and people
- Develop change management and adoption strategies
- Manage change request evaluation and approval process
- Coordinate stakeholder engagement and readiness activities
- Plan and execute training and support for end users
- Monitor adoption metrics and address resistance

### Goals
- Ensure smooth adoption of project deliverables
- Minimize disruption to business operations
- Maximize user acceptance and utilization
- Reduce resistance through proactive engagement

### Typical Communication
- Change impact assessments and readiness reports
- Training sessions and user support materials
- Stakeholder engagement workshops
- Adoption metrics and feedback analysis

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on change scope, timeline, and risk management
- **Product Manager**: Ensures change strategy aligns with product vision
- **Project Sponsor**: Secures support for change initiatives and resource allocation
- **Business Analyst**: Works together on process changes and user requirements
- **Communications Coordinator**: Coordinates change-related messaging and announcements
- **Stakeholders**: Engages users and manages adoption activities

## Role Interaction Matrix

The table below illustrates how the various roles interact and collaborate throughout the project lifecycle:

| Role | Primary Collaborators | Key Handoffs To | Key Handoffs From |
|------|----------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Project Manager** | All roles | Team Leads (execution), Communications Coordinator (updates) | Project Sponsor (direction), Business Analyst (requirements) |
| **Product Manager** | Project Manager, Business Analyst, Developers | Business Analyst (requirements), Developers (specifications) | Stakeholders (needs), Change Manager (feedback) |
| **Developers** | Product Manager, Quality Lead, Business Analyst | Quality Lead (builds for testing) | Business Analyst (specs), Product Manager (priorities) |
| **Project Sponsor** | Project Manager, Stakeholders | Project Manager (approvals), Change Manager (support) | Project Manager (status), Business Analyst (business case) |
| **Business Analyst** | Product Manager, Stakeholders, Developers | Developers (specifications), Quality Lead (test criteria) | Stakeholders (requirements), Product Manager (priorities) |
| **Quality Lead** | Developers, Business Analyst, Project Manager | Project Manager (quality status), Change Manager (release readiness) | Developers (deliverables), Business Analyst (acceptance criteria) |
| **Communications Coordinator** | Project Manager, All roles | Stakeholders (updates), All team members (meeting notes) | All roles (status updates, decisions) |
| **Change Manager** | Project Manager, Stakeholders, Business Analyst | Stakeholders (training), Project Manager (readiness status) | Business Analyst (process changes), Quality Lead (release info) |
